Every creative has a tool they are known for.

**A painter** has the brush.

**A photographer** has the camera.

**A writer** has the pen, or keyboard, depending on how dramatic they want to sound.

But for us digital **creatives**, the real tool of work is much <u>underrated</u>.

It is the *cursor*.

>Hidden in plain sight. Way too underrated.

That tiny arrow moving across the screen like it owns the place.

### Not just a pointer

It is the link between <u>thought</u> and <u>action</u>.

Before the design software matters, before the operating system matters, before the browser, before the tool stack, *there is always the cursor, waiting for instructions.*

### The tiny arrow that starts everything

It looks so simple that we barely think about it.

Just a tiny white arrow.

Sometimes black.

*Sometimes fancy, if you are ~~like me~~ the kind of person, who customizes everything.*

But that small arrow does alot more.

It `opens` the file.

`Selects` the image.

`Hovers` over buttons.

`Scrolls` for a living.

`Clicks` and summons things into existence. Reveals hidden menus. Deletes files into digital dust. And more...

### The cursor does not care what software you use

**This is the part I love.**

The cursor is loyal.

It does not care whether I am in **Photoshop** or **Canva**.

Or some website that promises to ~~compress~~ [squoosh](https://squoosh.app/) images better than every other website.

It just shows up.

Gets the job done.

- **In a design app**, it becomes a brush, a cutter, a mover, a ruler, a selector.
- **In a code editor**, it becomes a quiet little soldier, standing inside a line of code, waiting for the next command.
- **In the browser**, it becomes an explorer, every link a possible rabbit hole. Let's go hunting!
- **In the terminal**, well, the cursor becomes a blinking rectangle with attitude.

>Attitude?! Yeah, more on that in a bit... let the cursor do the blinking. Stay with me.

Different shape, same spirit.

It is always asking the same question.

*Where are we going next?*

Curiosity with coordinates.

Always there at the edge of the unknown, waiting for you to move.

I think that is why I love it.

Click here.

Open that.

What does this do?

What if I right click?

*That tiny arrow is the shape of curiosity on a screen.*

### The Designer

In line with my career choices, I spend most of my working life following that little arrow.

Every poster, website, logo, blog post, button, layout, edit, and curious journey begins with that movement.

*A little slide across the desk.*

Then suddenly, an idea starts becoming visible.

There is a rhythm to using the cursor.

Click.

Drag.

Stop.

Look.

Hover.

Select.

Move the design by two pixels.

Then another.

Stop again.

Then move it back because the first position had more soul.

Deselect.

Zoom in.

Zoom out.

Undo.

Redo.

*Because design is not always progress, sometimes it is controlled chaos.*

As a designer, you are not just moving objects.

You are moving attention.

You are moving meaning.

You are moving how someone will feel when they land on your design.

*Small arrow, big responsibility.*

### The attitude

Blink. Blink. Sometimes the cursor turns into a loading spinner, which is basically a way of saying, "<u>Hold on a moment, let me think.</u>"

Sometimes you click once and nothing happens.

So you click again.

Then again.

*Then suddenly seven windows open and now you are the problem.*

### The mouse in the room

**Hold my cursor**, and let me address the elephant in the room. The mouse! With all strings attached, the cursor and the mouse go hand in hand. Let that click!

And let us not even talk about **touchpads**.

A touchpad can humble a grown designer.

You think you are selecting text, then suddenly you have dragged an entire section into another dimension.

You think you are scrolling, oh no, you are zooming.

*This is why a good mouse is not just a device.*

It's my weapon of choice.

>As a designer, expect me to pull up to a gun fight armed with a mouse. Gamers do it!

### Where is my cursor

There is one place where the cursor loses its throne.

Android. ~~iOS who?~~

Phones are touch first.

No arrow.

*And while touch is beautiful in its own way, it is also different.*

Your finger is bigger than the thing you are trying to touch.

That tiny close button in the corner suddenly becomes a test of patience, precision, and spiritual maturity.

- **On desktop**, the cursor feels surgical.
- **On mobile**, tap, swipe, pinch, hold, hope.

A bit clumsy.

Maybe that is why serious creative work still feels different on a desktop.

Not because phones are weak.

Phones are powerful now.

But because the cursor gives you distance.

And distance gives you control.

The cursor lets you hover before you commit.

*Precision*.

### All cursors have a point

The more I think about it, the more I realize the cursor is not just a tool.

It is where ideas become decisions:

- Every click is a choice.
- Every drag a direction.
- Every hover a question.

Select what matters. Delete what does not.

*Every movement is a small vote for the version of the design you are trying to create.*

That is the creative process in miniature.

>And somewhere between the first click and the final export named something like "final-final-real-final-v23.png", the tiny cursor becomes more than a pointer.

It becomes proof that an idea moved.

### Respect the tiny arrow

So yes, my tool of work is the cursor.

For me, the cursor represents the whole creative life.

It represents:

- Movement.
- Curiosity.
- Control.
- Attention.
- Precision.

It is small, but it carries the weight of every digital craft we make.

A logo starts with it.

A blog post starts with it.

A website starts with it.

A brand direction starts with it.

*Even a random late night idea comes to life with that little arrow moving.*

### Long live the cursor

Mouse in hand, whatever you do.

Point.

Click.

Explore.

Break.

Learn.

Create.

Stay Curious,

Sham.
